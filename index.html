“No plan survives first contact with the enemy.” This common paraphrasing of Helmuth von Moltke applies as much to life and career as it does military affairs. After graduating from Duke, my initial plan was to work for a couple of years before going back to law school. However, after two years of work in a major NYC law firm and despite crushing the LSATs, I decided that the world didn’t need another lawyer - or at least it didn’t need me being one.

I took another legal job, but this time in a mutual fund company with the understanding that I wanted to move to research and portfolio management. I made that transition and this led to a career in money management - split roughly 50/50 between mutual funds and bank high net worth wealth management. Along the way, in an effort to constantly push myself and increase my knowledge and improve my skills, I completed the three-year Chartered Financial Analyst and Chartered Market Technician programs.

After taking some time off, I decided I wanted to go in a different direction. I had taken my first programming class back in high school and been an avid user of technology ever since. I embarked on an intensive 12-week bootcamp with Actualize which gave me experience with Ruby, Javascript, Vue.js, postgreSQL and more. Following the bootcamp, I was hired to be the TA for my instructor’s next cohort and have been active in Actualize’s post-bootcamp internship program.
